What are cookies?

Cookies are packets of data, that is sent to your computer from the server of websites you're visiting. Just like Hansel and Gretel in the fairytale, cookies leaves a trace after you on the internet. On most sites you visit, the server will send your web browser a cookie, that acts as you unique ID marker. Whenever you return to that same site, your browser will return the cookie to the server, and the server will know what traces your interactions with the site left on the web page last time.
Examples of traces can be

  • Login Credentials
  • Preferences (such as interface)
  • Items (in a online retailers shopping cart)

    • Cookies does no harm to your computer, as it is simply a package of information, sent back and forth between the server and your computer via your browser.
